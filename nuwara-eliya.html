Escape to Nuwara Eliya

Little England in the Hills

At an altitude of over 1,800 meters, Nuwara Eliya is the highest point of the Sri Lankan central plateau. Often called "Little England" for its cool, misty weather and Victorian-style bungalows, this highland city is the heart of the country's celebrated tea industry.

Rolling hills of emerald-green tea plantations, dramatic waterfalls like St. Clair's and Devon, and the tranquil Lake Gregory create a landscape that feels worlds away from the tropical coast. The crisp mountain air and the rhythmic sound of tea pluckers at work offer a serene and invigorating experience for every visitor.

Must-Visit Highlights

  • Tea Factory Tour
  • Gregory Lake
  • Horton Plains
  • Victoria Park
  • Post Office (1894)
  • Boat Rides
